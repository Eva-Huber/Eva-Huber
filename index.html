Sustainability

My passion for sustainability is rooted in my own life journey and relationship to the environment. I grew up with my feet in the dirt and my hands in the garden, always trying to grow something new or testing how different crops grew together in my backyard. With the short growing season living in Duluth, MN brings, moving to San Diego shocked me in the possibility of growth and opportunity to learn more about plants and the animals that support them. This led me to dive into policy work, volunteer opportunities and farming that all have increased my knowledge of the natural world, as well as Indigenous practices that care for and regenerate our relationship with the Earth.

Data Analyst

While working as a data analyst for SDSU office of sustainability I was a part of a team that values efficiency, adaptability, and above all else the planet and the people within it. This diverse team upheld high values and professionalism while advocating for the progression of cleaner campus energy and programs. Through analysis I was able to improve and increase accuracy of the universities energy database through monitoring and modifying the existing system, as well as streamlining it into one portfolio management system. This decreased energy bills as well as provided a better understanding of campus energy needs and usage.

Gardening Committee

As chair of the garden committee I was able to apply many of the community engagement and planning practices that align with my beliefs. Through my work I helped begin a new community garden in collaboration with the College Area Baptist Church, as well as taught peers how to successfully grow their open gardens, even in limited spaces. Seeing students' surprise at how an onion grows reinforced my mission to educate people about where our food comes from. Food insecurity in San Diego has the highest rate in the SDSU region, so combatting this through practical approaches students could take helped to increase student food security and knowledge.

SDSU Green Love

As an active member of Green Love my four years of school I helped in the organization of two climate strikes, one having over 1,000 participants, wrote climate policy to be passed by the sustainability senate, designed and ran an eight week climate justice lecture series, and worked with the university to pressure sustainable growth as they expanded through their EIR for Mission Valley Campus West.

Farming

In both Hawaii and Washington I have worked on start up permaculture and organic farms, starting their idea development and program build outs. These experiences have both reinforced the importance of native planting and working with crops that grow well within the area you are working for strengthening environmental resilience and long-term yield

Hawaii

In Hawaii on the big island I created an internship program that developed interns into knowledgeable permaculture farmers through hands-on experiences on the two plots of land that the owner had. Here I helped to create garden design layouts and teach new interns about permaculture principles that framed our farm.

Washington

In Washington I assisted in startup processes of an organic farm focused on regenerative practices. Here I did soil tests and worked to add natural amendments to increase soil productivity and health as well as increase resistance to storms and bugs.

Service

I was recruited to SDSU through the Vice President of Student Affairs service and leadership scholarship, based on my previous community service and leadership roles. This program offered extensive leadership training through workshops, conferences, mentorship and hands-on experience training peers on valuable skills learned through my leadership knowledge. This program all culminated in the development of a food security program that I designed and implemented in my senior year. The program provided a multitude of current resources to students in Green Love, as well as the general student body through workshop training, and posters through workshop training, and posters.

Work Experience

Since I was 14 years old I have worked in the service industry to support myself financially. This started as making protein shakes, which led to managing the business with supervision of the owner by the time I was 16. I have worked in a couple of different restaurants throughout college in MN and WY. In WY I also worked as a tour guide and a lift operator. I was able to provide customers with an engaging dialogue that was both informative and interesting about the surrounding geographical area. As I have continued my career as a lift operator in CA, I have always ensured safety as well as providing the best service possible in any weather condition, all with a smile and a positive attitude. While leading other lift operators I uphold teamwork and communication as my key values and believe to ensure everyone is doing a good job they must be heard and respected.

My role in the service industry has provided financial stability throughout college while also teaching me the importance of kindness and respect in any environment to ensure the best experience for my coworkers and guests.

Volunteering

SInce I was a kid, I have spent many days volunteering in churches, community centers and food banks. I find the time spent in these places some of the most formative experiences I have had, as reminders of the good of people in the communities I live in and of the human nature we all inherent. Whenever I have moved somewhere new, the first thing I look for is a place to volunteer, as I believe this is a great way to engage with your community and give back to people who could use an extra hand, since we all have been at points in our life where a little support makes all the difference. In San Diego I spent time at Urban Angels, and most recently in Tahoe I have been volunteering with Sierra Community House in their food distribution program.
