Experience

Bryant Farm and Forest

After completion of my bachelors degree I accepted a job as the director of operations at Bryant Farm and Forest, a permaculture farm in Washington state. Here I oversaw and implemented the seed rotation and planting layout for the 1.5 acre vegetable plot. During my time there I tested soils, applied nutrients to the overgrown lot to begin the soil regeneration process, started hundreds of seeds for fall planting, and designed zones 0, 1 and 2 around the property’s house in accordance with permaculture zones.

Office of Sustainability Data Analyst

As San Diego State University's energy and data analyst intern I updated and oversaw the energy tracking database, managed the university’s portfolio platform switch to Energy Star, encouraged certification of Green Labs, created events to engage students in sustainability and implemented ideas to create more efficient and sustainable energy usage; such as adding timers to building lights and heating periods.

Permaculutre Internship

My first internship in permaculture and agriculture planning was an immersive experience in Hilo, Hawai’i. During this program I had the opportunity to develop a garden layout for the main house, manage rental properties and do field work involving reintroducing native plants to the property teaching me the interdependency we have in our ecosystem.

Tree San Diego

My first internship was as the community education and project manager intern with Tree San Diego. During my 6 month internship I developed educational content and workshops for children to connect with nature through art, collected Field Maps data regarding San Diego’s tree coverage for a report on the effect of urban heating in historically disadvantaged communities and coordinated volunteer efforts to plant trees all around San Diego. This organization opened my eyes to the impact our disconnection to nature has on our urban cities and inspired me to dive into agriculture.
